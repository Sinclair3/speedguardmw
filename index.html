⚙️ One-time Setup
Connect Live Tracking
To see other drivers on the map in real-time, paste your Firebase project details below. This is free and takes 5 minutes to set up.
⚠️ Why this is needed: Live tracking requires a database that all phones write to and read from. Firebase (by Google) is free for this purpose. Without it the app still works for speed trap alerts — just no live drivers.
Step 1
Go to firebase.google.com → Sign in → Click "Create a project" → Name it "speedguard-malawi" → Disable Google Analytics → Click Continue.
Step 2
In your project → Click "Realtime Database" (left sidebar) → Click "Create Database" → Choose any location → Select "Start in test mode" → Click Enable.
Step 3
Go to Project Settings (gear icon top left) → Scroll to "Your apps" → Click "Web" (</>) → Register app → Copy the config values into the fields below.

SpeedGuard Malawi — Terms of Use v1.0

1. Purpose. SpeedGuard Malawi is a road safety awareness tool to help drivers on the M1 stay informed about speed limits and reported enforcement zones between Lilongwe and Blantyre.

2. Not an evasion tool. This app does NOT encourage speeding or evading law enforcement. You remain fully responsible for obeying all Malawi traffic laws at all times.

3. Location data & live tracking. By using this app you agree your approximate GPS position, chosen nickname, and speed will be shared in real-time with other users. This data is not stored permanently and is only transmitted while the app is open with GPS enabled.

4. Community reports. Trap locations submitted by users may not be accurate. Always drive within legal limits regardless of reported trap positions.

5. Privacy. Only your nickname and approximate location are visible to others. No personal information (name, phone number, national ID) is collected or permanently stored.

9. Updates. Terms may be updated at any time. Continued use constitutes acceptance.
